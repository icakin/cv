Dissertations


PhD: Understanding microbial and geochemical factors contributing to the wastewater treatment performance in constructed wetlands; a case study within scotch whisky distilleries (PhD Adviser: Dr Mark Taggart, Dr Barbara Morrissey, Dr Lucio Marcello, Dr Paul Gaffney)

 

The dissertation focuses on four main research areas: 1) Optimising eDNA isolation and analysis of wetland gravels to maximise community profile information. 2) Designing a biochar-based pre-treatment method for distillery effluents. 3) Effects of substrate on treatment performance and microbial community in mesocosms. 4) Long-term performance and microbial community monitoring of constructed wetlands treating whisky distillery effluents.

 

MSc: The effect of salinity and shaking on growth, and lipid biosynthesis-related gene expression in Parachlorella kessleri (MSc Adviser: Dr Ethan Hack)

 

The dissertation focuses on two main research areas: 1) Quantification and characterisation of lipid molecules of green algae Parachlorella kessleri, under different stress inducing conditions such as salinity and agitation. 2) Gene expression profile (accD and rbcL) of Parachlorella kessleri, under the same stress inducing conditions.
